Comments on Andy's review paper

Hi guys -- thanks for offering to review sections of my paper. Here's the link to get to it. It's password-protected, so you'll have to ask me for the password.

Here's the deal. I am writing this up to submit to Review of General Psychology. Here are the details, taken from the site:

  • Review of General Psychology seeks to publish innovative theoretical, conceptual, or methodological articles that cross-cut the traditional subdisciplines of psychology.

  • The journal contains articles that advance theory, evaluate and integrate research literatures, provide a new historical analysis, or discuss new methodological developments in psychology as a whole.

  • Review of General Psychology is especially interested in articles that bridge gaps between subdisciplines in psychology as well as related fields or that focus on topics that transcend traditional subdisciplinary boundaries. Authors are encouraged to write their manuscripts from the perspective of more than one subdiscipline and to review literature that spans at least two subdisciplines.

  • Manuscripts are of particular interest to Review of General Psychology when they provide a provocative challenge to customary or prevailing views; intellectual risk-taking is encouraged. Articles devoted primarily to reporting new empirical findings are generally not appropriate for the journal.

I'm going to update my document with 10 pages or so every week as I revise the full draft that's already on my computer. Your job, if you're willing, is to let me know what's wrong with everything.

In particular I'm looking for a "lean-back" read on it. What seems way too detailed? What doesn't make any sense? What made you write (or think) a lot of question marks while you were reading it? Am I meeting the goals of the journal?

Please make your comments on the electronic copy of the document if you can. My preference is that you not rewrite huge swaths of text (use a comment instead) unless you have to.

Thanks for your time, and mail me with any questions! You can also leave a comment here and I'll get it.
