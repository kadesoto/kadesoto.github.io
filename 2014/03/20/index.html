Redoing a figure#

Every once in a while we researchers have the pleasure of recreating a historic or classic figure for reuse in a presentation or paper. It's a zenlike experience to engage in this process of re-transcription. Today's mission was to redo Figure 1 from a 1962 paper by Bennet Murdock () on the serial position curve in memory.#

Here's the original figure...#

#

... and here's my redo.#

#

Although the new figure is much more flexible (can be edited, etc.) and vector rather than raster (can be resized, reshaped, etc., without loss of quality), there's a real elegance to the original figure. In particular, I bumped into issues recreating the "shiny" bubbles of the 20-1 condition in the original, so I had to settle for a less interesting (but perhaps more readable) intermediate gray shade. Additionally, it's not so easy to create those "legend arrows" from the original, so I stuck with a more traditional legend.#

What did the original figure creator get right? What did he or she get wrong? I was about to assume the figure was Murdock's work, but it occurred to me that the journal typesetter or editor may have created the figure. I think I have seen similar figures in papers by other authors, but am not certain.#

P.S.: I utilize a feature of Fargo, my outlining system, for the first time in this post. Above, I link to Murdock's paper but, like so many other academic works, it's behind a paywall.
