March 17

Catching up

Getting back into the swing of things this Monday. Last week was spring break here at WUSTL, so I worked from home a bunch and got some professional development-related things going for a change of pace. I was expecting to segue back into my more usual work when I got sidelined with a weird bug (perhaps from something I ate Friday night?) and was on my back all weekend as a result. My brain is only now starting to clear up.

Some neat links for the day: These things called Mod Notebooks -- you pay $25, get a notebook in the mail, and then ship it in when it's filled and they digitize it for you. On one hand $25 seems pretty low (especially considering a traditional Moleskine's worth least half that without the digitization included). But on the other hand it seems much more useful to get something digitized on a daily basis. Once I fill up a notebook, what are the chances I'm going to want to go back and look at it?

Also, if you're interested in my March Madness bracket, come on and get me right here.

I spent the morning working on a review for a journal article I received over the weekend. I can't say much about it here but will share it around if and when it's published. Reading through I was reminded just how hard it can be to write a good paper. It's such a particular challenge to keep the overall goal of a manuscript in mind (e.g., the main thesis, how sections connect to each other) at the same time you keep the underlying facts accurate and concise. Writing really is a balancing act.

The biggest challenge is getting my head back into my dissertation, which I've taken a break from the last few days. It's helpful going back to look at some of my previous posts (1), (2) to refresh my memory. The next step is seeing if any critical elements are missing from my prior analyses and then going forward with the proposed Experiment 3.

(Last note -- you might have noticed that this site is now hosted on my Technimentis domain. I've always felt this is the right place for documenting and writing about my research.)
