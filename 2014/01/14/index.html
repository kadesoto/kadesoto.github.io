Puzzling over calibration curves

So I've put together an unusual type of calibration curve for Experiment 1 of the DeSoto and Roediger (2014) paper. In fact, I've spent all morning thinking about it. Here's what it looks like.

In my experiment, subjects make a confidence rating from 0 (not at all confident) to 100 (entirely confident) after making a recognition decision (i.e., "old" or "new"). We divide up the confidence scale into new responses and old responses. Then, perfect calibration means expected accuracy with a zero confidence rating is .50. (Previously, when we lumped "old" and "new" responses together, expected accuracy was zero. Or wait, maybe it wasn't.)

I need to do a bit more research on calibration curves in general to understand why I'm getting tangled up in what should be a straightforward concept.
