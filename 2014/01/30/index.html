January 30

Meeting with Roddy

Met to discuss Experiment 2 of Project 15 and showed off a lot of the figures I've been working on over the last few weeks. We agreed it was time to go ahead and progress beyond the pilot phase and collect another 52 subjects' worth of data. I'll see if any lab members are available to assist with the data collection, but I hope to complete it in February. Could be tough -- that is almost three people per day for the month -- but I think we can do it.

Some recommendations I got or we talked about:

  • I should check to see if the relatively high guess rate for related lures (M = .14) is in the auditory or visual condition (or both).

  • I should also keep a careful eye out for the number of observations in different cells, and perhaps show these in figures and tables where the concern applies.

  • I could use items as the method of analysis in the proportion of remember, know, and guess tables.

  • I need to think more about what the confidence-accuracy plots actually mean when we subdivide by remember, know, and guess. Being able to explain these plots -- and really dig into them -- is going to be a critical part of the dissertation.

We also briefly discussed "media strategy" for DeSoto and Roediger (2014) and whether I'll be able to get help from any undergraduate students over the next few months.

Last, I just opened 75 slots on Experimetrix. We'll see who we get next week. I'd better make sure my study is rock solid!
