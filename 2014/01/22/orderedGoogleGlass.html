Ordered Google Glass -- any potential for research?

Not technically a research note, but today I ordered a tangerine-colored Google Glass that should arrive in a few days. It's not really a purchase I can afford (not being a developer, there's not much of a financial incentive reason to get one), so I imagine it'll be going back within the 30-day return window.

I don't consider myself a "Glasshole," so I'm nervous about the social repercussions of wearing them around and about -- not to mention safety (can I wear them on a bus? jogging around the neighborhood?) -- so it'll be interesting, if not a little anxiety inducing, to play around with them for a few days. Maybe it won't be as bad as I'm imagining, especially on the campus of an elite private school where everyone and their mother has a top-of-the-line Macbook Pro.

Bottom line is, if you'd like to play around with them for a little bit, come see me in a week or so. And if you see me with them on, please aim the rocks at my cushy bits and not my face. I can afford the doctor's bill but definitely can't afford the Glass replacement.

But -- can anyone think of any interesting memory studies to be done with them? If so, now is the time to hit me up.
